HABITUAL E-COMMERCE MOBILE APP.

Problem

A recent survey found that the online e-commerce industry struggles with usability when discovering products online. I wanted to make online e-commerce solutions much more user friendly. As an entrepreneurial fanatic I toke the initiative to use AI technology to solve this problem in the industry.

Platform: Mobile App Design.

Role played

I was an entrepreneurial UX/UI Designer within a virtual Agile environment working with a teammate. I contributed remotely and the tasks were discussed in Trello during standups. I was responsible for the aesthetics and the UX including planning of the project and using this information to make the challenge reality. This concerned an entrepreneurial case and I used it to spearhead my design choice.

Skills performed: Design thinking, motion design, design system, sketches, user flows, site maps, high & low fidelity wireframes, prototypes, visual design, LEAN Startup.

Solution

Assuming every generation knows how to perform a text search, I added a search bar to find products easily. Moreover, I personalized the search results by adding a screen with Interests. The algorithm can then personalize the homepage with personalized recommendations (to ensure that visitors find more of what they want, and enhances the site experience to drive customer loyalty), including recent browser history.

Research

The research proved that older users get flustered by more complicated websites, while younger users tend to favor websites with a UX that is visual and intuitive in nature, which would repel less tech-savvy users.

Challanges

Recreating the wheel with an effective search pattern rather than reoccurring solutions that solve common design problems is risky. Older generations are more averse to pattern changes than younger generations. Nevertheless, after analysis of the user steps using user flow I decided to keep the basic structure of the navigation and replaced the navbar with browsing history and personalized recommendations.

Impact

Adding personalized recommendations increase the chance of remembering and buying what the users like. The clunky product category buttons were replaced into a header menu, which simplified the navigation area and improved its readability. This way Habituals navigation becomes more streamlined.

Lessons learned

During launch phase I’ve realized how important it is to constantly tweak the site to align with buyer motivations. For example, I’ve learned that if customers want our product both cheaper and recommended, it is good to emphasize text such as “50% OFF” or “STAFF PICK” with i.e. bold black text. Constantly testing of pages helped me to grow and retain a loyal customer base.
